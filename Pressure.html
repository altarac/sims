The Particle Balancing Act

A balloon's size is the result of a constant balancing act between tiny air particles.

  • Blue particles inside push the balloon outward.
  • Red particles outside squeeze the balloon inward.

The balloon expands or shrinks until the number of internal "pushes" matches the external "squeezes." Scientists call this equilibrium.

Use the sliders to change density or speed, and use the thermometer to probe the environment. Watch how the balloon reacts to find a new balance!
