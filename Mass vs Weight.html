What's the difference?

In everyday language, we often use the words "mass" and "weight" to mean the same thing, but in science, they are very different!

Mass

Is a measurement of how much matter is in an object. It's an intrinsic property. If you go to the moon, you don't lose any of your atoms, so your mass stays the same!

Weight

Is a measurement of force. It's how hard gravity is pulling down on that mass. Less gravity means less pull, which means less weight.

Right now, gravity on Earth is pulling on the astronaut with an acceleration of 9.81 m/s². This creates a downward force (weight) of 686.7 Newtons (or 154.4 lbs).
