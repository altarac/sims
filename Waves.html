What's happening?

In a Longitudinal Wave (like sound), particles oscillate parallel to the direction the energy travels, creating areas of high pressure (compressions) and low pressure (rarefactions).

In a Transverse Wave (like light or water ripples), particles oscillate perpendicular (up and down) to the direction the energy travels, creating crests and troughs.
