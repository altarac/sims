Physics Reference

Lenses follow the thin lens equation using the Cartesian sign convention:

$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u}$$

Convex (Converging)

Focal length $f$ is positive.

  • $|u| > f$: Real, inverted image.
  • $|u| < f$: Virtual, upright, magnified image.

Concave (Diverging)

Focal length $f$ is negative.

  • Always produces a virtual, upright, and diminished image.
  • The image is always located between the lens and the focal point.
