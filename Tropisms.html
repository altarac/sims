Tropisms in Action

A tropism is a biological phenomenon indicating growth or turning movement of a biological organism, usually a plant, in response to an environmental stimulus.

Phototropism Positive

Notice how the green stem bends and grows towards the light source. Plants produce a hormone called auxin that redistributes to the shaded side, causing those cells to elongate faster and bending the plant towards the light for maximum photosynthesis.

Hydrotropism Positive

Observe the roots seeking out the blue water patches. Roots will grow towards areas of higher moisture concentration. This survival mechanism ensures the plant can absorb the water necessary to live.

Geotropism (Gravitropism)

Negative (Stem)

Even without a light source, the stem naturally grows upwards, defying gravity to reach the surface.

Positive (Roots)

Roots naturally grow downwards into the soil, pulled by gravity, which helps anchor the plant and reach deep nutrients.
